7 Best AI Sermon Transcription Apps in 2026

April 8, 2026 · 12 min read

The best AI sermon transcription apps in 2026 are: Sermon Keeper (best overall for churchgoers), TurboScribe (best free option for existing recordings), Castmagic (best for content repurposing), SermonlyAI (best for Bible reference detection), Notta (best for multilingual churches), Rev (best for publication-quality accuracy), and VEED (best for video sermons). The right choice depends on whether you're recording live, transcribing existing files, or need publication-ready accuracy.

We tested seven tools on real sermon recordings to compare accuracy, speed, biblical vocabulary handling, and value. Here's what we found.

2. SermonlyAI — Best for Bible Reference Detection

SermonlyAI focuses specifically on sermon AI analysis. Beyond transcription, it automatically identifies Bible references in the transcript, analyzes sermon themes, and tracks spiritual growth insights over time.

Key features: AI transcription with automatic Bible reference detection. Sermon theme analysis. Spiritual growth tracking. PDF export. Searchable sermon library.

General-Purpose Tools That Work for Sermons

3. TurboScribe — Best Free Option for File Transcription

TurboScribe uses OpenAI's Whisper model to deliver 99.8% accuracy — the highest among AI-only tools. Its free tier is remarkably generous: 3 files per day, up to 30 minutes each. That's 90 minutes of free transcription daily.

Key features: Upload MP3, WAV, MP4, or paste a YouTube URL. 98+ language support. Speaker identification. Multiple export formats (TXT, PDF, SRT, DOCX).

Transcription quality: Excellent on general accuracy. Handles most biblical names well due to Whisper's large training data. Scripture references may appear as plain numbers (e.g., "Romans 828" instead of "Romans 8:28").

Pricing: Free: 3 files/day (30 min each). Paid: $10/month (annual) or $20/month for unlimited.

Pros: Best free tier for file transcription. Very high accuracy. Fast processing. Supports nearly 100 languages.

Cons: No Bible-specific features. No sermon summaries. Web-only — no mobile app. No live recording capability.

4. Castmagic — Best for Sermon Content Repurposing

Castmagic goes beyond transcription — it turns one sermon recording into blog posts, social media captions, newsletters, show notes, and more. If your church needs to repurpose sermons across multiple channels, Castmagic automates the entire workflow.

Key features: AI transcription with 60+ language support. Automatic content generation: blog posts, social clips, key quotes, episode summaries. Custom templates for recurring workflows. Speaker identification.

Transcription quality: Good on general speech. Not optimized for biblical vocabulary, but the content generation features compensate by producing polished outputs that can be edited.

Pricing: Free trial available. Hobby: $23/month (annual). Starter: $59/month (annual).

Pros: Turns one sermon into 10+ content pieces automatically. Great for church media teams. Custom templates save hours of weekly content creation.

Cons: Expensive for individual use. Content-creator focused, not churchgoer focused. No Bible-specific features. Web-only — no mobile recording.

5. Notta — Best for Multilingual Churches

Notta supports 58 languages with real-time translation, making it ideal for multilingual congregations. If your church has services in Spanish, Korean, Mandarin, or other languages, Notta handles the transcription and can translate between languages.

Key features: 58 language support. Real-time transcription. AI summaries. Speaker identification. Mobile and web apps.

Transcription quality: Good across languages. English accuracy is on par with other general tools. Non-English sermon accuracy depends on the language but is generally reliable.

Pricing: Free: 200 min/month (5 min per recording). Pro: $8.17/month (annual).

Pros: Best multilingual support. Affordable. Good AI summaries. Mobile apps available.

Cons: Free tier limits recordings to 5 minutes — unusable for sermons without upgrading. No Bible-specific features. Less accurate than TurboScribe on English content.

6. Rev — Best for Publication-Quality Accuracy

Rev offers both AI and human transcription. The AI option is affordable for regular use, while the human option delivers 99% accuracy with transcriptionists who can handle theological content — ideal when the transcript will be published.

Key features: AI transcription ($0.25/min). Human transcription ($1.99/min, 99% accuracy). Caption and subtitle generation. Mobile app for recording.

Transcription quality: AI is comparable to other tools (~95%). Human transcription is significantly better for biblical content, with transcriptionists who can verify Scripture references and spell theological terms correctly.

Pricing: Free: 45 min/month AI. AI: $0.25/min (~$11 for a 45-min sermon). Human: $1.99/min (~$90 for a 45-min sermon).

Pros: Human option for highest accuracy. Trusted brand (15+ years). Good for churches publishing transcripts. Both AI and human options.

Cons: Expensive for regular use, especially human transcription. AI transcription is not sermon-specific. Pay-per-minute adds up quickly for weekly sermons.

7. VEED — Best for Video Sermons

VEED is primarily a video editing tool with strong transcription built in. If your church posts sermon videos on YouTube or social media, VEED can transcribe the video, add subtitles, and let you clip highlights — all in one workflow.

Key features: Video transcription and subtitles. 125+ languages. Auto-generated captions. Video editing tools. Social media export formats.

Transcription quality: Good for clear video audio. Accuracy drops with poor audio quality or heavy background music. No Bible-specific optimizations.

Pricing: Free: ~10 min of subtitles. Basic: $18/month. Pro: $30/month.

Pros: Best for churches that need video subtitles. Huge language support. All-in-one video workflow.

Cons: Expensive for transcription-only use. Video-focused — overkill if you just want text. No Bible features. No mobile app for recording.

How to Choose the Right Tool

Choose Sermon Keeper if: You want to record and transcribe during church in one step, with Bible verse detection and sermon summaries. Best for individual churchgoers on iPhone.

Choose TurboScribe if: You already have sermon recordings (MP3, YouTube) and want accurate, free transcription. Best for processing existing files.

Choose Castmagic if: Your church media team needs to turn one sermon into blog posts, social media clips, newsletters, and show notes automatically.

Choose SermonlyAI if: You want deep Bible reference detection and sermon theme analysis beyond basic transcription.

Choose Notta if: Your church offers services in multiple languages and you need transcription in Spanish, Korean, Mandarin, or other non-English languages.

Choose Rev if: The transcript will be published on your church website or in print, and you need professional-grade accuracy with theological proofreading.

Choose VEED if: You need to add subtitles to sermon videos for YouTube or social media.
